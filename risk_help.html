What is a Risk?

Within the context of a software development project, a risk is any possible future event that could negatively impact the software project. This could be in regards to the project schedule, product quality, customer satisfaction, or many other factors. Unexpected future events, changes to the software requirements, and choices made by the project team can all risk the success of the project in some way.

Risks can be fairly minor, perhaps only causing a few days of delay in the project schedule, or they can be catastrophically major, causing the entire project to fail, or any level in between. They also range in likelihood of occurring, some being very probable while others are only a small chance. As many risks as possible should be identified for a software project, but considering limited time and resources, only a certain number, valued first based on high probability and second based on dangerous effects, should be selected for further analysis and development of risk mitigation, monitoring and management strategies.
